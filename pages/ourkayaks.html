There are pure touring boats, where the most important thing is the load capacity and the solvency in windy conditions and waves can maintain a high cruising speed being loaded and under adverse conditions; these boats tend to be the longest in length. There are others oriented to the competition where the objective is the highest possible speed, then load capacity and comfort are sacrificed when working in waves and wind. There are also school boats and recreational boats, which give preference to the integral stability of the boat, thinking of an inexperienced paddler or a family group that could include, for example, a pet, where the boat must have the ability to absorb poorly distributed weights or sudden changes , without being in danger. These types of boats give great satisfaction and fun, and have a varied load capacity and performance. This means that we have very safe boats and are also capable of traveling a long distance in a short time.
